Steve

Tongji University, Shanghai, China

Currently, I am a PhD student majoring in environmental engineering at Tongji University, and my main research scope is membrane-based water treatment technologies.

Before coming to Shanghai, I was a master's student at Hubei University in Wuhan. During this time, my studies focused on the ultrafiltration (UF) process, including using UF to remove manganese ions from underground water, and controlling membrane fouling in this process. The three years of studying and living in Wuhan are unforgettable memories, with happiness, hardships, and a touch of regret. Anyway, I learned solid lab skills here and met buddies from all over the country.

About me, I haven't figured out how to introduce myself yet. Since I just became a junior researcher this year, I am still learning all of my professional skills. Becoming a PhD student is an exciting thing for me, and I can't imagine the challenges I'll encounter over the next few years. Maybe I can make a tiny gains in the water treatment field, or get stuck in a rut and accomplish nothing, who knows! As a record of my journey, I started to blog about these meaningful things, while also trying to learn programming.
